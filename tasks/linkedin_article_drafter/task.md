# LinkedIn Article Drafter — Long-Form Pulse Article

Use the browser to navigate to LinkedIn's article editor, draft a long-form professional article on a technical leadership topic, format it with proper structure, and publish or save it as a draft.

## Topic
Write a LinkedIn article titled: **"From IC to Tech Lead: 5 Lessons I Learned the Hard Way"**

## Steps

### 1. Navigate to LinkedIn Articles
- Go to `https://www.linkedin.com`
- If you're already logged in, proceed
- If a login page appears, **stop immediately** — report and exit
- Click "Write article" (usually under the "Start a post" box, or at `https://www.linkedin.com/article/new`)
- Wait for the article editor to fully load

### 2. Set Up the Article
- **Title**: "From IC to Tech Lead: 5 Lessons I Learned the Hard Way"
- **Subtitle/description field** (if available): "The transition from individual contributor to tech lead isn't a promotion — it's a career change. Here's what nobody told me."
- Add a **cover image** suggestion in the content (note: the AI can't upload images, but should note where one should go)

### 3. Write the Article Body

Structure the article with these sections:

```
[COVER IMAGE PLACEHOLDER]
<!-- SUGGESTION: A split-screen image showing code on the left, a team whiteboard on the right -->

## The Moment Everything Changed
*Opening hook — a specific anecdote about the first time you realized writing code wasn't your primary job anymore. 150-200 words.*

## Lesson 1: Your Calendar Is Now Your IDE
*How time management replaced code reviews as your main tool. Scheduling, context-switching, protecting maker time. 150-200 words.*

## Lesson 2: The Best Code Is the Code You Don't Write
*Delegation, trusting the team, letting go of technical decisions. The art of asking questions instead of giving answers. 150-200 words.*

## Lesson 3: 1:1s Are Your Most Important Meeting
*Building relationships, understanding motivations, unblocking people. How 30 minutes a week transforms team performance. 150-200 words.*

## Lesson 4: Communication Scales Faster Than Architecture
*Writing RFCs, giving clear feedback, managing up and sideways. Why your words now matter more than your commits. 150-200 words.*

## Lesson 5: You're Not Supposed to Have All the Answers
*Vulnerability as leadership, asking for help, creating psychological safety. The best leads say 'I don't know' regularly. 150-200 words.*

## The Bottom Line
*Closing paragraph — 100 words tying the lessons together with a forward-looking statement.*

---

**What was your hardest lesson transitioning to leadership? Drop it in the comments.**

#techleadership #engineeringmanagement #careergrowth #softwareengineering #fromictolead
```

### 4. Format the Article
- Use LinkedIn's built-in formatting: headings (H1/H2), bold, italic, bullet lists
- Each section should have a clear heading
- Paragraphs: 3-4 sentences max for readability
- Total article: 1200-1800 words
- Include 5 hashtags at the end

### 5. Review and Publish
- Preview the article (if LinkedIn offers preview mode)
- Check for formatting issues, spelling, and flow
- **Before publishing**: take a screenshot of the editor
- Click "Publish" (or "Save as draft" if configured for review-only)
- Confirm the article appears on your profile under "Articles"

### 6. Report
Return:
- The article title and word count
- A summary of each section (one sentence each)
- Screenshot of the editor before publishing
- The published article URL (if published) or confirmation of draft save
